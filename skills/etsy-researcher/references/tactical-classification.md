# Tactical Classification

Use this reference when classifying Etsy listings into tactical demand tiers.

## Core Principle

Classify from evidence that is actually visible in the collected dataset.
Do not infer momentum, conversion quality, or demand durability from vibes alone.

## Exclusion Rules

Exclude a listing from tactical recommendations when any of the following is true:
- Clear IP or trademark risk appears in the title, imagery description, or listing framing
- The pattern appears stale or dead based on the available evidence
- The listing lacks enough data to support a meaningful classification
- The listing is irrelevant to the target niche or buyer intent

## Tactical Tiers

### Defender
Stable but saturated or low-upside patterns.
Typical signals:
- High review count but weak freshness signals
- Repetitive wording across many sellers
- Commodity presentation with little differentiation
- Mature demand with limited whitespace

### Midfielder
Usable patterns with room for adaptation.
Typical signals:
- Clear buyer intent
- Moderate competition
- Some differentiation opportunities in angle, material, bundle, or audience
- Viable search demand without obvious breakout momentum

### Forward
High-priority attack opportunities.
Typical signals:
- Strong buyer-facing clarity
- Distinct angle or sub-niche positioning
- Evidence of fresh traction, premiumization, or under-served demand
- Reusable text angles with room for multiple listing variants

## Dead Pattern Heuristic

Treat a pattern as suspect or dead when:
- Review volume is high but there are no visible freshness signals
- The market is crowded with near-identical copy and no obvious whitespace
- The niche is built on weak trend residue rather than durable demand

Do not overstate certainty when freshness data is limited.
Instead, label confidence as low, medium, or high.

## IP / Policy Filter

Remove listings that rely on:
- Franchise names
- Trademarked labels or mechanics
- Character likenesses marketed as unofficial clones
- Logos, music rights, or obvious protected branding

## Reporting Guidance

For every recommended niche or angle, explain:
- Why it fits Defender, Midfielder, or Forward
- What evidence supports that classification
- What risks or uncertainties remain
