# Tactical Classification

Use this reference when classifying Etsy listings into tactical market buckets.

## Core Principle

Classify from evidence that is actually visible in the collected dataset.
Use sales, review, badge, freshness, and query-position clues when available.
Do not infer momentum or durability from vibes alone.

## Tactical Buckets

### Defender
Evergreen patterns that sell steadily across the year with lower volatility.

#### Strong signals with extension data
- Very high total sold
- Still shows recent sales activity such as Sold 24H
- Continues converting instead of surviving on historical reviews alone

#### Strong signals without extension data
- Ranks near the top when using highest-review sorting or equivalent high-social-proof signals
- Shows very large review counts such as 1k or 5k+
- Appears consistently prioritized by Etsy search

#### Anti-pattern
Do not classify a dead pattern as Defender.
If review count is high but there is no evidence of fresh sales, weakening visibility, or the pattern no longer shows current demand, exclude it from Defender recommendations.

### Midfielder
Seasonal, event-driven, or cyclical patterns that surge around recurring demand windows.

#### Typical signals
- Title or keyword framing clearly ties to an upcoming event, season, or recurring shopping moment
- Strong buying signals such as Sold 24H, Bestseller, or other fresh marketplace momentum
- Demand appears linked to a specific calendar window rather than year-round stability

#### Common examples
- Valentine
- Halloween
- Back to school
- Graduation
- Election-themed or event-driven gift cycles

### Forward
Short-term breakout or trend patterns that can spike hard and fade fast.

#### Strong signals with extension data
- Recently launched or newly visible pattern
- Unusually strong Sold 24H relative to listing age or review count
- Evidence of fast breakout momentum within a short 30-60 day window

#### Strong signals without extension data
- Sort by newest listings or equivalent date-desc signals
- Listing is relatively new
- Review count is still low, often under 50
- Etsy already gives it a strong badge such as Bestseller or Popular Now

#### Interpretation
Treat Forward patterns as attack opportunities, not long-term foundations.
Exploit quickly, validate fast, and avoid overcommitting until durability is clearer.

## Dead Pattern Rule

Treat a pattern as dead or suspect when:
- Review count is high but there is no visible fresh-sales evidence
- The listing no longer appears strongly in the market read
- The niche looks crowded with stale copy and weak new momentum

When confidence is limited, mark the pattern as uncertain instead of pretending certainty.

## Confidence Rules

### High confidence
Use when multiple signals align, such as:
- review strength
- badge signal
- fresh-sales signal
- freshness or listing-age clue
- strong search placement

### Medium confidence
Use when only some signals are visible and the classification is plausible but incomplete.

### Low confidence
Use when key signals are missing because Etsy, tooling, or the browsing environment limits extraction.
Do not make hard claims from low-confidence reads.

## IP / Policy Filter

Exclude listings that rely on:
- franchise names
- trademarked terms or mechanics
- logos or protected brand identifiers
- unofficial clones that are obviously piggybacking on protected IP

## Reporting Guidance

For every recommended niche or angle, explain:
- tactical bucket: Defender, Midfielder, or Forward
- confidence level: high, medium, or low
- visible evidence supporting the classification
- risks, including IP, saturation, dead-pattern risk, or weak freshness data
