# Language Safety

Use controlled uncertainty language for military and geopolitics scripting.

## Always Prefer

- may have
- could have
- might
- reportedly
- analysts suggest
- according to open-source reporting
- sources indicate
- it is believed that
- satellite imagery suggests

## Avoid

- confirmed
- according to CENTCOM
- according to official sources
- it is confirmed that
- facts show
- the truth is

## Principle

Fast-moving conflict reporting is often incomplete, contested, or shaped by propaganda.
Use language that preserves uncertainty while still allowing vivid narration.

## Application

- Use hedge language when describing strikes, casualties, deployments, battlefield gains, timelines, and strategic intent.
- If the scenario includes hypothetical escalation, make that status clear through wording rather than explicit disclaimers inside every sentence.
- Never frame speculation as settled fact.
